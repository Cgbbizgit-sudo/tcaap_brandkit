01 · Positioning

A venture studio, not a collective.

tCAAP is the venture studio building Cebu into the Philippines' arts and animation capital. It bridges local creative talent to global opportunity through a full ecosystem: education, enterprise, events, creative media services, and original content.

What the brand has to carry

tCAAP sits in decks next to DTI, CIE British School, Core Works Force, Kasagi Labo, and partner studios. The mark has to hold its own in that company. It cannot look like an arts club, a student project, or a streaming service. It has to read as infrastructure. As a platform. As something institutional enough for government, disciplined enough for Japanese and Korean clients, and warm enough for the artists it exists to serve.

Brand personality

VoiceConfident, direct, ecosystem-minded. Speaks in commitments, not aspirations.
PostureInstitutional but creative. Serious without being cold.
Position versus peersThe only player with the full ecosystem: Township, Education Partner, Production Events, Creative Media Service, Original Content. Everyone else does one or two. tCAAP does all five.
Cebu as identityCore pillar. Cebu is not a location tag, it is the thesis. The brand should feel grounded in Cebu without becoming a tourism visual.
What to avoidHeritage pastiche, tropical clichés, generic creative gradients, anime mascots, tech startup neon.
02 · Primary Mark

The Gateway.

An institutional archway containing a five-step ascent. The outer form is the gateway: tCAAP as the threshold between local talent and global opportunity. Inside the gateway, a staircase climbs from the ground floor on the left to a gold beacon at the top right. Each step is one of the five tCAAP pillars. The climb is the brand thesis made visible.

Primary mark · on navy

What the mark means

Every element is load-bearing. Read the mark out loud, element by element, and the brand story tells itself.

The archway

The outer shape is an institutional threshold. A gateway, a doorway. It says tCAAP is the doorway: not a school, not a studio, not a collective, but a threshold organization that exists so people pass through it and come out the other side changed. The arched form carries institutional weight without slipping into corporate blandness. It reads cleanly whether it lives next to DTI's seal or next to Kasagi Labo's wordmark. It is the frame that holds everything else.

The staircase

Inside the archway, a staircase climbs from bottom-left to top-right. This is where the brand story happens. The bottom-left is the ground floor: where a Filipino artist or animator begins, untrained or undiscovered or underpaid, with no platform and no path. The top-right is the destination: global opportunity, a real career, an international client, a place in the industry. The space between those two points is the gap the tagline promises to bridge, and the staircase is the literal act of bridging it. It is the one thing Kasagi Labo, Core Works Force, and every competing studio cannot claim inside a mark: a visible commitment to the journey.

The five steps

The steps are not five for design reasons. They are the five tCAAP revenue pillars in ascending order: Arts and Animation Township, Artist Collective Enterprises, Production Events, Creative Media Service, and Original Content Production. Each step is one program. Each program is one rung in the climb. Someone who enters tCAAP at the Township, trains in the Collective, performs at Events, crosses into Media Services, and produces Original Content has walked every step in the mark. The logo encodes the business model.

The gold beacon

At the top of the staircase, a single rectangle of Signal Gold marks the destination. It is the only non-neutral color in the entire brand system, which means it carries all the emphasis. It sits above the final step, naming where the journey ends. Signal Gold also does quiet cultural work: warm, echoing Filipino heritage without flag imagery or tropical tourism clichés, and giving the whole institutional grey-and-navy system a pulse of humanity.

What the mark refuses

The absences are as deliberate as the presences. The mark has no characters or mascots, because tCAAP is not an animation studio but the institution that feeds them. No gradients or chrome, because the previous CP logo had all three and aged immediately. No cultural ornaments, no Spanish colonial arches, no Sinulog colors, no sun rays, no beach imagery, because tCAAP is infrastructure, not tourism. No embedded wordmark, because the icon must stand alone on favicons, merchandise, embroidery, and stamps. Every absence is a decision.

The one-line version · for pitching

The archway is tCAAP. The staircase inside is our five programs. The gold at the top is the opportunity we get our people to. Everything else is just the climb.

05 · Clear Space & Minimum Size

Protection rules.

Clear space is measured in units of the n leg width (the vertical thickness of the outer archway). One unit of clear space is required on all four sides. No typography, photography, or competing graphic elements enter the clear space zone. When in doubt, double it.

Minimum sizes

Digital · primary mark40px height minimum. Below this, the gold beacon stops reading cleanly.
Digital · faviconUse the mono navy or mono white version. Gold beacon omitted for clarity at 16px.
Print · primary mark12mm height minimum. Below this, use the all-black version for reproduction safety.
Horizontal lockup120px or 35mm minimum width.
Tagline lockup180px or 50mm minimum width. Below this threshold the tagline becomes illegible and must be dropped.

08 · Usage Rules

What the brand protects.

Every rule here exists because the previous mark broke one. If the brand survives its first eighteen months without these violations, the identity compounds instead of eroding.

DO

Use deep navy as the default background for investor decks, pitch material, and digital properties. The brand is built dark-mode first.

DON'T

Apply metallic gradients, chrome effects, or bevels to the mark. The previous CP logo used all three and aged immediately. This system is flat.

DO

Keep the gold beacon on the top step of the staircase as the only accent inside the mark. It signals the opportunity endpoint and earns its position.

DON'T

Add rainbow gradients, multiple accent colors, or decorative overlays. Every added color weakens the gateway concept.

DO

Pair the mark with substantial whitespace. Clear space is a minimum, not a target. Give the brand room to carry authority.

DON'T

Stretch, skew, rotate, outline, or flip the mark. The staircase ascends left to right for a reason. Mirroring it reverses the meaning from ascent to descent.

DO

Use Steel Blue for decorative elements: dividers, shape overlays, supporting graphics on navy backgrounds.

DON'T

Set text in Steel Blue on Navy. The contrast ratio fails WCAG. Use Paper, Mist Light, Mist, or Gold for text on navy.
